What Is an AI Engineer?

The first node of the roadmap — and the one that defines every lesson after it. Phase 2 of your curriculum starts here.

Where you are
Lessons 9–21 made you a harness engineer — you can make an AI agent reliable. From here, the AI Engineer roadmap widens the lens: you'll learn to build AI-powered applications against any model. This lesson is the map's legend.

The Definition

An AI Engineer builds applications on top of pre-trained foundation models — they don't train the models from scratch. The job is to take a model someone else trained (GPT, Claude, Gemini, Llama) and turn it into a working product feature: a chatbot, a search system, an agent, a document summarizer.

"An AI Engineer uses pre-trained models and existing AI tools to improve products and solve real-world problems — rather than building models from the ground up." — roadmap.sh/ai-engineer

This is a relatively new role, made possible by the API era of LLMs. Before ~2023, "doing AI" meant collecting data, choosing architectures, and training. Now the capability is a phone call away — an HTTP request — and the hard part is everything around the model: prompts, context, retrieval, tools, evaluation, safety, cost.

The key distinction
The ML Engineer asks "how do I make a model that's good at X?" The AI Engineer asks "given models that are already good at many things, how do I compose them into a reliable product?" You are firmly on the second path — and you've already mastered its hardest sub-discipline (the harness).

AI vs AGI — and why it matters to your job

Today's models are narrow AI: extraordinarily capable within the distribution of their training, but without genuine general reasoning or goals. AGI (Artificial General Intelligence) — a system that matches human flexibility across any task — does not exist as of 2026.

Why this matters practically: every engineering decision you make assumes a model that is powerful but bounded. It will hallucinate, it has a knowledge cutoff, it has a context limit, it has no persistent memory unless you give it one. The entire discipline of AI engineering — RAG, tools, evals, the harness you already know — exists to work around the gap between narrow AI and AGI.
